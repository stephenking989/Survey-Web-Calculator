01 Curve Parameters
▼
SymbolNameDescription
R
ft / m		 Radius Radius of the circular curve. Larger radius = gentler curve.

ft / m		 Sub Tangent Distance from BC/PC along the tangent to the intersection point (PI).

ft / m		 External Distance from PI to the midpoint of the curve, measured perpendicular to the chord.

02 Primary Curve Formulas
▼
Chord
C = 2 × R × sin(Δ/2)
Chord length from radius and half the delta angle.
Sub Tangent (Tangent)
T = R × tan(Δ/2)
Distance along tangent from BC to PI (or PI to EC).
Midordinate
M = R × (1 − cos(Δ/2))
Distance from chord midpoint to arc midpoint.
External
E = R × (1/cos(Δ/2) − 1)
Distance from PI to the curve, perpendicular to the chord.
Arc Length
L = (π × R × Δ) / 180
Arc length = radius × delta angle in radians.
Degree of Curvature (Arc)
Dₐ = 5729.58 / R
Degree of curvature based on arc definition (arc length = 100).
Degree of Curvature (Chord)
Dₒ = 2 × sin⁻¹(50 / R)
Degree of curvature based on chord definition.
03 Supplementary Calculations
▼
Radius from Midordinate
R = M / (1 − cos(Δ/2))
Calculate radius when midordinate and delta angle are known.
External from Radius
E = R × (1/cos(Δ/2) − 1)
Calculate external distance from radius and delta angle.
PI to Centre Distance
O→PI = √(R² + T²)
Distance from the curve centre to the point of intersection.
External (Alternative)
E = O→PI − R
External from PI-to-centre distance minus radius.
Delta in Radians
Δ = L / R
Delta angle in radians from arc length and radius.
Delta in Degrees
Δ = (L / R) × (180° / π)
Delta angle in degrees from arc length and radius.
04 Chainage & Deflection
▼
Chainage Relationship
PI − T = BC    BC + L = EC
Relationship between PI, tangent, begin curve, arc length, and end curve stations.
Deflection Angle
δ/2 = (90 × l) / (π × R)
Deflection angle (degrees) for a given arc length l measured from BC along the curve.
05 Trigonometry Reference
▼
Basic Trig Ratios
sin(α) = Opposite / Hypotenuse
cos(α) = Adjacent / Hypotenuse
tan(α) = Opposite / Adjacent
Angle Conversion
Degrees to Radians
rad = deg × (π / 180)
Radians to Degrees
deg = rad × (180 / π)
Law of Sines
sin(α)/a = sin(β)/b = sin(γ)/c
Angle form — use to find an unknown angle.
a/sin(α) = b/sin(β) = c/sin(γ)
Distance form — use to find an unknown side.
Law of Cosines
Find side a
a² = b² + c² − 2bc × cos(α)
Find side b
b² = a² + c² − 2ac × cos(β)
Find side c
c² = a² + b² − 2ab × cos(γ)
Find angle α
cos(α) = (b² + c² − a²) / (2bc)
Find angle β
cos(β) = (a² + c² − b²) / (2ac)
Find angle γ
cos(γ) = (a² + b² − c²) / (2ab)
06 Distance Calculations
▼
Horizontal Distance from Slope & Angle
HD = SD × sin(α)
Horizontal distance from slope distance and vertical angle.
Horizontal Distance (Pythagorean)
HD = √(SD² − VD²)
Horizontal distance from slope distance and vertical distance.
Slope Distance
SD = √(HD² + VD²)
Slope distance from horizontal and vertical distances.
Vertical Distance from Zenith
VD = SD × cos(Z)
Vertical distance from slope distance and zenith angle Z.
Pythagorean Theorem
a² + b² = c²
Relationship between sides of a right triangle.
Hypotenuse
c = √(a² + b²)
Calculate hypotenuse from the two shorter sides.
Side from Hypotenuse
a = √(c² − b²)
Calculate one side when hypotenuse and other side are known.
! Important Notes
▼
›All angle calculations assume degrees unless specified as radians.
›Ensure consistent units throughout — all feet or all metres.
›The curve calculator requires 2–3 known parameters to solve for the rest.
›PI = Point of Intersection  |  BC / PC = Begin Curve  |  EC / PT = End Curve.
›Use Settings in the Curve Calculator to adjust decimal precision and angle format (DMS / DD).
