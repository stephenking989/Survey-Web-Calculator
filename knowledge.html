02 Primary Curve Formulas
▼
Chord
C = 2 × R × sin(Δ/2)
Chord length from radius and half the delta angle.
Sub Tangent (Tangent)
T = R × tan(Δ/2)
Distance along tangent from BC to PI (or PI to EC).
Midordinate
M = R × (1 − cos(Δ/2))
Distance from chord midpoint to arc midpoint.
External
E = R × (1/cos(Δ/2) − 1)
Distance from PI to the curve, perpendicular to the chord.
Arc Length
L = (π × R × Δ) / 180
Arc length = radius × delta angle in radians.
Degree of Curvature (Arc)
Dₐ = 5729.58 / R
Degree of curvature based on arc definition (arc length = 100).
Degree of Curvature (Chord)
Dₒ = 2 × sin⁻¹(50 / R)
Degree of curvature based on chord definition.
03 Supplementary Calculations
▼
Radius from Midordinate
R = M / (1 − cos(Δ/2))
Calculate radius when midordinate and delta angle are known.
External from Radius
E = R × (1/cos(Δ/2) − 1)
Calculate external distance from radius and delta angle.
PI to Centre Distance
O→PI = √(R² + T²)
Distance from the curve centre to the point of intersection.
External (Alternative)
E = O→PI − R
External from PI-to-centre distance minus radius.
Delta in Radians
Δ = L / R
Delta angle in radians from arc length and radius.
Delta in Degrees
Δ = (L / R) × (180° / π)
Delta angle in degrees from arc length and radius.
04 Chainage & Deflection
▼
Begin Curve Station
BC = PI − T
Begin curve chainage from PI station and sub tangent.
End Curve Station
EC = BC + L
End curve chainage from begin curve station and arc length.
Deflection Angle
δ/2 = (90 × l) / (π × R)
Deflection angle (degrees) for a given arc length l measured from BC along the curve.
05 Trigonometry Reference
▼
Basic Trig Ratios
sin(α) = Opposite / Hypotenuse
cos(α) = Adjacent / Hypotenuse
tan(α) = Opposite / Adjacent
Angle Conversion
Degrees to Radians
rad = deg × (π / 180)
Radians to Degrees
deg = rad × (180 / π)
Law of Sines
sin(α)/a = sin(β)/b = sin(γ)/c
Angle form — use to find an unknown angle.
a/sin(α) = b/sin(β) = c/sin(γ)
Distance form — use to find an unknown side.
Law of Cosines
Find side a
a² = b² + c² − 2bc × cos(α)
Find side b
b² = a² + c² − 2ac × cos(β)
Find side c
c² = a² + b² − 2ab × cos(γ)
Find angle α
cos(α) = (b² + c² − a²) / (2bc)
Find angle β
cos(β) = (a² + c² − b²) / (2ac)
Find angle γ
cos(γ) = (a² + b² − c²) / (2ab)
06 Distance Calculations
▼
Horizontal Distance from Slope & Angle
HD = SD × sin(α)
Horizontal distance from slope distance and vertical angle.
Horizontal Distance (Pythagorean)
HD = √(SD² − VD²)
Horizontal distance from slope distance and vertical distance.
Slope Distance
SD = √(HD² + VD²)
Slope distance from horizontal and vertical distances.
Vertical Distance from Zenith
VD = SD × cos(Z)
Vertical distance from slope distance and zenith angle Z.
Pythagorean Theorem
a² + b² = c²
Relationship between sides of a right triangle.
Hypotenuse
c = √(a² + b²)
Calculate hypotenuse from the two shorter sides.
Side from Hypotenuse
a = √(c² − b²)
Calculate one side when hypotenuse and other side are known.
07 Compound Curves
▼
Compound Curve Parameters
SymbolNameDescription
Rl
ft / m		 Large Radius Radius of the larger-radius sub-curve.
Rs
ft / m		 Small Radius Radius of the smaller-radius sub-curve.
Δ
degrees		 Total Delta Total deflection angle = Δl + Δs.
Δl
degrees		 Delta (Large Curve) Deflection angle for the large-radius sub-curve.
Δs
degrees		 Delta (Small Curve) Deflection angle for the small-radius sub-curve.
Tlong
ft / m		 Long Tangent Distance from main PI to BC1 — the longer of the two main tangents.
Tshort
ft / m		 Short Tangent Distance from main PI to EC2 — the shorter of the two main tangents.
Tl
ft / m		 Sub-tangent (large) Tangent length along the Rl curve from BC1 to PCC.
Ts
ft / m		 Sub-tangent (small) Tangent length along the Rs curve from PCC to EC2.
PCC Point of Compound Curvature The point where the two sub-curves meet.
BC1 Begin Curve Start of the compound curve, on the large-radius side.
EC2 End Curve End of the compound curve, on the small-radius side.
Key Relationship
Total Delta Angle
Δ = Δl + Δs
The total deflection angle is the sum of the two sub-curve delta angles.
Main Tangents
Long Tangent
Tlong = [ Rl − Rs·cos(Δ) + (Rl − Rs)·cos(Δs) ] / sin(Δ)
Distance from the main PI to BC1. Requires Rl, Rs, Δ, and Δs.
Short Tangent
Tshort = [ Rl − Rs·cos(Δ) − (Rl − Rs)·cos(Δl) ] / sin(Δ)
Distance from the main PI to EC2. Requires Rl, Rs, Δ, and Δl.
Solving for Delta Angles — arccos form
Δl from Ts (arccos)
Δl = cos−1( 1 − [ Ts·sin(Δ) − Rs·(1 − cos(Δ)) ] / (Rl − Rs) )
Solve for the large-curve delta angle given Ts, Rl, Rs, and Δ.
Δs from Tl (arccos)
Δs = cos−1( 1 − [ Rl·(1 − cos(Δ)) − Tl·sin(Δ) ] / (Rl − Rs) )
Solve for the small-curve delta angle given Tl, Rl, Rs, and Δ.
Solving for Delta Angles — arctan form (alternate)
Δl from Ts (arctan)
Δl = 2·tan−1( [ Ts·sin(Δ) − Rs·(1−cos(Δ)) ] / [ Tl + Ts·cos(Δ) − Rs·sin(Δ) ] )
Alternate form — algebraically equivalent to the arccos version above.
Δs from Tl (arctan)
Δs = 2·tan−1( [ Tl·sin(Δ) − Rl·(1−cos(Δ)) ] / [ Ts + Tl·cos(Δ) − Rl·sin(Δ) ] )
Alternate form — algebraically equivalent to the arccos version above. (Symmetric counterpart to the Δl arctan form — verify against source if results differ.)
Solving for Radii
Large Radius from Rs, Ts, Δ, Δl
Rl = Rs + [ Ts·sin(Δ) − Rs·(1−cos(Δ)) ] / (1−cos(Δl))
Solve for the large radius when Rs, Ts, Δ, and Δl are known.
Small Radius from Rl, Tl, Δ, Δs
Rs = Rl − [ Rl·(1−cos(Δ)) − Tl·sin(Δ) ] / (1−cos(Δs))
Solve for the small radius when Rl, Tl, Δ, and Δs are known.
08 Traverse Coordinates
▼
Departures from HD & Azimuth
Northing Departure
ΔN = HD × cos(Az)
Northing departure from horizontal distance and azimuth. Az is a full-circle azimuth in degrees (0–360).
Easting Departure
ΔE = HD × sin(Az)
Easting departure from horizontal distance and azimuth.
Delta from Two Points
Northing Delta
ΔN = N2 − N1
Northing change: End point minus Start point.
Easting Delta
ΔE = E2 − E1
Easting change: End point minus Start point.
Coordinate Propagation
Northing of Point B
NB = NA + ΔN
Compute northing of next point from known point and departure.
Easting of Point B
EB = EA + ΔE
Compute easting of next point from known point and departure.
09 Traverse Closure & Adjustment
▼
Closure Error
Linear Error of Closure
LEC = √(ΣΔN² + ΣΔE²)
Resultant misclosure vector from the sum of northing and easting errors.
Ratio of Precision
RoP = ΣHD / LEC
Traverse precision expressed as a ratio (e.g. 1 : 5000 — higher is better).
Compass Rule Adjustment
Correction Factor — Northing
CFΔN = ΣΔN / ΣHD
Proportional correction factor distributed across all northing departures.
Correction Factor — Easting
CFΔE = ΣΔE / ΣHD
Proportional correction factor distributed across all easting departures.
Compass Rule Correction — Northing
CR = CFΔN × HDab
Northing correction for leg ab, proportional to its length.
Compass Rule Correction — Easting
CR = CFΔE × HDab
Easting correction for leg ab, proportional to its length.
10 Line Geometry
▼
Slope-Intercept Form
y = mx + b
Line expressed using slope m and y-intercept b.
Point-Slope Form
(y − y1) = m(x − x1)
Line through a known point (x₁, y₁) with slope m.
Slope of a Line
m = (y2 − y1) / (x2 − x1)
Gradient calculated from two known points on the line.
General Form
Ax + By + C = 0
Any line in general (standard) form. Solve for x, y, or any coefficient given the others.
